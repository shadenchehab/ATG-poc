Why this PoC matters

Derek is a one-man analytics shop fielding ad-hoc revenue questions from leadership with no analyst support. Salesforce and NetSuite don't talk - every cross-sell, wallet-share, or bookings-to-revenue question requires manual exports, Excel reconciliation, and hours of rebuild.

"He's spending hours on work that should take minutes, across systems he can only partially access himself."

Since Blackstone's acquisition in January 2026, the frequency and urgency of leadership questions has increased. His approval to hire analysts has been revoked. He needs a force multiplier, not a hire.

Use cases

Revenue validation & reporting
Out of box
  • Total closed-won revenue by service line for Q1 2026
  • Top 10 accounts by closed-won dollar volume in Q1 2026

Proves the SF connection works and Computer returns accurate revenue data. Validates against native SF report within ±1%.

Operational & pipeline analytics
Out of box
  • Average deal size by service line, trended QoQ (last 5 quarters)
  • YTD win rate by service line (closed-won / total closed)
  • Average days from creation to close for Q4 2025 deals
  • Time in "Initiate Contact" and "Assess" stages by service line

Demonstrates Computer replaces ad-hoc SF reports. Multi-dimensional analysis without pre-built dashboards.

Bookings to Billings foundation
Out of box
  • Distribution of Q1 2026 close dates (Jan vs. Feb vs. Mar)
  • Average lag from creation to close by service line, year-over-year
  • Average lag from close date to anticipated start date
  • Current open pipeline by service line and expected close date

Forward-looking revenue modeling foundation. Proves Computer can support the Bookings-to-Billings questions leadership asks.

BU bucketing & sub-service-line parsing
Custom skill
  • "What's Q1 revenue by BU?" (groups service lines into BU buckets)
  • "Break down ACS into sub-service lines" (e.g., CEMS Integration vs. CEMS Service)
  • "How does CEMS Integration pipeline compare to CEMS Service?"

Encodes ATG's business taxonomy so users ask naturally at BU or sub-service-line level without re-explaining mappings each session. This is what Claude cannot do persistently.

Risks & mitigations

Stage history not available in SF sync - Cannot answer time-in-stage questions (UC2.4)
Pre-validate during setup; fallback to estimated duration from snapshot timestamps
"Anticipated Start Date" field missing or sparse - UC3.3 partially blocked
Confirm existence upfront; if sparse, flag data quality gap to Derek
Service line values inconsistent - Typos/variations break BU grouping logic
Pull distinct values early; build normalization into the skill

Decision criteria

  • Is it meaningfully better than their current Claude workflow - not just a lateral move?
